ITR Filing

Income Tax Return (ITR) is a form used to file information about your income and tax to the Income Tax Department. It is mandatory for individuals and businesses to file ITR if their income exceeds a certain threshold. Our experts help you file your ITR correctly and on time.

Required Documents for ITR Filing

Filing your Income Tax Return (ITR) requires different documents depending on whether you are a salaried individual, a business owner, or a freelancer.

Here is a comprehensive checklist to ensure you have everything ready before the deadline.

1. Essential Personal Documents

These are mandatory for every taxpayer:

  • PAN Card: Your Permanent Account Number.
  • Aadhaar Card: Must be linked with your PAN to file successfully.
  • Bank Account Details: You need the Account Number and IFSC Code for all active bank accounts. (One must be selected for your refund).

2. For Salaried Individuals

If you earn a salary, your employer is your primary source of documentation:

  • Form 16: This is the most important document. It is a certificate issued by your employer showing your total salary and the TDS (Tax Deducted at Source).
  • Monthly Salary Slips: Useful to verify allowances like HRA, LTA, etc.
  • Rent Receipts: If you stay in a rented house and want to claim HRA tax exemption.

3. Financial Statements (Income from Other Sources)

  • Bank Passbook/Statement: To calculate interest earned on Savings Accounts.
  • Interest Certificates: From banks or post offices for Fixed Deposits (FDs).
  • Form 26AS & AIS (Annual Information Statement): You can download these from the IT portal. They show all tax deducted on your behalf by banks, employers, or clients.

4. Investment Proofs (For Deductions under Section 80C to 80U)

If you want to reduce your tax liability under the Old Tax Regime, you need:

  • Life Insurance (LIC) Premium receipts.
  • Public Provident Fund (PPF) passbook entries.
  • ELSS (Mutual Funds) investment statements.
  • Health Insurance Premium (Section 80D) receipts for self and parents.
  • Home Loan Interest Certificate: Issued by the bank (Section 24).
  • Education Loan Interest certificates.
  • Donation Receipts: For Section 80G claims.

5. For Business Owners & Professionals (ITR-3 or ITR-4)

If you are running a shop or working as a freelancer:

  • Profit & Loss Statement and Balance Sheet.
  • GST Turnover: Your total sales as per GST returns.
  • Cash Position: Closing cash-in-hand and bank balances.
  • Invoices & Expense Receipts: For rent, electricity, staff salary, and marketing.

6. Capital Gains (Stocks & Property)

  • Consolidated Tax Report: If you trade in the stock market (available from Zerodha, Upstox, etc.).
  • Property Sale Deed: If you sold land or a house during the year (includes purchase price, sale price, and registration costs).

Important Dates to Remember

  • Salaried/Individuals: Usually July 31st of the assessment year.
  • Businesses (Audit cases): Usually October 31st.
