Trade License

A trade license is a document that permits an individual or a business to operate a business in a particular area. It is issued by the local municipal corporation. Our service simplifies the process of obtaining a trade license for your business.

Trade License Requirements

1. Individual & Identity Proofs

  • Aadhaar Card of the applicant.
  • PAN Card (Personal PAN for Proprietors, Business PAN for Companies/Firms).
  • Recent Passport Size Photographs of the applicant/partners/directors.
  • Voter ID / Driving License (Secondary ID).

2. Proof of Business Premises

This is the most important set of documents for a Trade License:

  • If Self-Owned: Latest Property Tax Receipt OR Sale Deed OR Allotment Letter.
  • If Rented: Registered Rent Agreement AND a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the landlord.
  • If Leased: Lease Deed.
  • Utility Proof: Latest Electricity Bill or Water Bill (must be in the name of the owner).

3. Business Constitution Documents

  • For Partnership Firms: Partnership Deed.
  • For Companies: Certificate of Incorporation (COI), Memorandum of Association (MOA), and Articles of Association (AOA).
  • For LLPs: LLP Agreement.

4. Additional Documents (Industry Specific)

Depending on what your business does, you may need these permits:

  • Fire NOC: For restaurants, warehouses, etc.
  • Health Trade License: For food-related businesses.
  • Pollution NOC: If your business creates noise or waste.
  • Site Plan: A layout map of the premises.
  • Udyam/GST Registration: As supporting business proof.

Important Tips for Application

  • Validity: Most Trade Licenses are valid for 1 year and must be renewed annually.
  • Penalties: Operating without a license can lead to fines or business closure.
  • Zoning: Ensure your business is in a commercial zone.
